Unmanned Surface Vehicle
USV-5
Autonomous Surface Patrol and Survey Vehicle
Development

The USV-5 is a 5.2-meter catamaran-hull unmanned surface vehicle designed for coastal patrol, hydrographic survey, and environmental monitoring. The twin-hull configuration provides superior stability in beam seas compared to monohull designs of equivalent length, enabling reliable sensor operation at sea state 4 without stabilization hardware.

Propulsion is via twin outboard electric motors with full redundancy. Navigation is handled by a tightly coupled GS-2 INS/GNSS module with AIS transponder integration. The modular amidship payload bay supports sensor configuration changes without returning to a maintenance facility - antenna arrays, sonar transducers, and optical systems mount via a standardized rail and connector system.

Communications use the CM-4A module for encrypted line-of-sight links, supplemented by Iridium satellite for over-the-horizon operations. An autonomous collision avoidance system implements COLREGS-compliant behavior for safe operation in mixed maritime traffic.

Mission Profiles
  • Coastal border patrol and intrusion detection
  • Hydrographic and bathymetric survey
  • Oceanographic data collection (CTD, water sampling)
  • Port and harbor perimeter security
  • Search and rescue coordination platform
  • Persistent environmental monitoring

Autonomous Underwater Vehicle
UUV-2A
Underwater Survey and Inspection Vehicle
Development

The UUV-2A is a torpedo-form autonomous underwater vehicle designed for subsurface environmental mapping, infrastructure inspection, and bathymetric survey. Its pressure-rated aluminum hull is rated to 200 m depth, covering the continental shelf and near-offshore infrastructure that represents the primary survey target for Moroccan maritime operations.

Propulsion is a single rear thruster with two vectorable bow thrusters for three-axis maneuvering capability - enabling station-keeping in moderate currents and precise positioning for inspection tasks that pure torpedo-form AUVs cannot achieve. The modular nose section accepts interchangeable payload configurations without drydocking.

The GS-2 inertial guidance module provides navigation during GPS-denied submerged operation. A Doppler velocity log and acoustic positioning transponder supplement inertial navigation for long-duration missions. Surface communication uses acoustic modem while submerged, switching to radio on surfacing.

Mission Profiles
  • Bathymetric seafloor mapping and charting
  • Submarine cable and pipeline inspection
  • Port and harbor approach survey
  • Environmental baseline and monitoring surveys
  • Offshore structure inspection (piers, breakwaters)
  • Mine detection survey (with appropriate sensor payload)
